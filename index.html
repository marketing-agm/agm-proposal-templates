AGM is experienced in navigating both the Multifamily Tax Exemption (MFTE) and Mandatory Housing Affordability (MHA) programs, advising Ownership on eligibility, implementation, and the strategic value of participation.

MFTE
Multifamily Tax Exemption
A Washington State program providing a property tax exemption on the residential improvements of a multifamily development in exchange for reserving a portion of units at affordable rent levels. Qualified properties receive a meaningful reduction in annual property tax liability for a term of 8 or 12 years, varying by jurisdiction.
Washington State Program8 or 12-Year TermAffordable Unit Set-AsideReduced Tax Liability
MHA
Mandatory Housing Affordability
A City of Seattle program requiring new development in certain zones to include affordable units on-site (the performance option) or contribute to the city’s affordable housing fund (the payment option). Compliance unlocks density bonuses and development flexibility in qualifying zones.
City of SeattlePerformance OptionPayment OptionDensity Bonus Access
Applicability
Where & When the Programs Apply
MFTE is available in participating cities across Washington — including Seattle, Bellevue, Kirkland, Redmond, and Renton — with terms varying by jurisdiction. MHA applies within designated zones in the City of Seattle. Both are typically implemented at the time of new development or substantial rehabilitation.
SeattleBellevueKirklandRedmondRenton
Implementation
Application & Ongoing Compliance
Implementation includes confirming eligibility, filing the MFTE application, designating affordable units, executing a restrictive covenant with the city, and managing annual compliance — income certifications and ongoing reporting — alongside standard property operations.
Eligibility ReviewMFTE ApplicationRestrictive CovenantAnnual Compliance
Strategic Value Property tax savings, compliance efficiency, and development flexibility — managed within standard property operations
